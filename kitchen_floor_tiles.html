Kitchen Floor Tiles for Modern Living

Your kitchen floor sets the tone for the entire space. The right tile balances practicality — slip resistance, easy cleaning, long-term durability — with the design statement you want to make. Our collection spans classic marble and handcrafted zellige to bold geometric encaustic and urban concrete-effect porcelain.

Choose large-format tiles for a seamless, expansive feel in open-plan kitchens, or opt for mosaic and patterned formats to add character and texture to compact spaces. Every tile in this collection is selected for quality, longevity, and visual impact.

1
Surface Preparation

Ensure the subfloor is clean, dry, and level. Remove any old adhesive or debris. A smooth base guarantees lasting adhesion.

2
Dry Layout

Dry-fit tiles from the centre of the room outward before applying adhesive. This ensures balanced cuts at the edges and symmetrical patterns.

3
Apply Adhesive

Use a notched trowel to apply high-quality tile adhesive. Work in small sections (1–2 sq m) to prevent the adhesive from drying before placement.

4
Set Tiles & Grout

Press tiles firmly with a slight twist. Use spacers for consistent joint width. Allow adhesive to cure for 24 hours before grouting.

5
Seal & Finish

Apply appropriate sealant if specified for the finish type. Buff clean and allow full cure before foot traffic (typically 48–72 hours).
