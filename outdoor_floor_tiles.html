1
Surface Preparation

Ensure the subfloor is clean, dry, and level. Remove any old adhesive or debris. A smooth base guarantees lasting adhesion in high-traffic commercial areas.

2
Dry Layout

Dry-fit tiles from the centre of the room outward before applying adhesive. This ensures balanced cuts at edges and symmetrical patterns.

3
Apply Adhesive

Use a notched trowel to apply high-quality tile adhesive. Work in small sections (1–2 sq m) to prevent the adhesive from drying before tile placement.

4
Set Tiles & Grout

Press tiles firmly with a slight twist. Use spacers for consistent joint width. Allow adhesive to cure for 24 hours before grouting.

5
Seal & Finish

Apply appropriate sealant for the finish type. Buff clean and allow full cure before heavy commercial foot traffic (typically 48–72 hours).
